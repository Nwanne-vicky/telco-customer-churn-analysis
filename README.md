# Telco Customer Churn Analysis

**Author:** Victoria Nwanne  
**Role:**   Data Analyst  

---

## Project Overview

This project presents an end-to-end analysis of customer churn within a telecommunications company. The objective was to identify key factors influencing customer attrition and provide actionable insights to improve retention.

The project combines SQL for data preparation, Power BI for visualization, and Canva for communicating insights through a structured presentation.

---

## Tools & Technologies

- SQL (Data cleaning & transformation)
- Power BI (Dashboard development & visualization)
- Canva (Presentation & storytelling)
- Excel (Initial data handling)

---

## Project Workflow

### 1. Data Cleaning & Preparation (SQL)

The raw dataset required significant cleaning before analysis. Using SQL, the following steps were carried out:

- Removal of duplicate records
- Standardization of categorical values
- Handling of missing and inconsistent data
- Conversion of data types for accuracy and consistency
- Structuring the dataset for analysis

This ensured the dataset was reliable and analysis-ready.

---

### 2. Data Analysis & Visualization (Power BI)

An interactive **3-page dashboard** was developed to explore churn patterns and key business metrics.

#### Dashboard Structure:

**Page 1: Revenue Drivers**
- Total customers
- ARPU
- Revenue insights

**Page 2: Revenue at Risk**
- Revenue at Risk by Contract
- Churn % by Internet Service and Tech Support
- Avg Revenue (Churned)

**Page 3: Who Churned**
- Identification of high-risk customer segments
- Service-related churn patterns
- Behavioral indicators of churn

---

### 3. Insight Presentation (Canva)

To effectively communicate findings, a presentation was created using Canva. The presentation translates technical analysis into business-focused insights.

It covers:
- Data cleaning approach (SQL)
- Key churn drivers
- Customer behavior patterns
- Business recommendations

---

## Key Insights

- Customers on month-to-month contracts showed significantly higher churn rates
- Lack of services such as tech support and online security increased churn likelihood
- Fiber optic users exhibited higher churn compared to other internet service types
- Higher monthly charges were associated with increased customer churn

---

## Business Recommendations

- Encourage long-term contracts through incentives
- Improve customer support services to reduce churn risk
- Review pricing strategies for high-cost plans
- Target high-risk segments with retention campaigns
